# The Regulars — Project description

*Target length for the application form: ~250 words.*

---

*"The Regulars"* is a wall-hung interactive sculpture: twelve round eyes
of varying sizes, clustered in a dark wooden frame roughly 900 × 600 mm,
that turn their gaze toward whoever is standing in front of them. Walk
past it and every eye follows you — not as a mirror, but from its own
position in the frame, so the cluster physically converges on your face
the way a room of regulars might look up when the door opens.

The piece is about the soft, social edge of attention. Bars are already
places where strangers perform for one another; *The Regulars* makes the
audience literal. A seated patron glances up and finds twelve
hand-painted eyes fixed on them, blinking occasionally, pupils widening
as they approach, drifting into a kind of patient idleness when the
room is still. Two people passing each other will see the eyes hand
them back and forth. A crowded night turns the piece into a quiet
chorus of witnesses; an empty afternoon leaves the eyes looking toward
the door, waiting.

Each eye is a round display built individually from raw components,
driven by a custom-built electronic nervous system concealed inside the
frame. The piece plugs into a single wall outlet, makes no sound, and
installs on a standard French cleat. At Madrone Art Bar, it is designed
to live above the bar itself, so every drink ordered is witnessed by
the regulars who never leave.
