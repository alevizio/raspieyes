# Artist bio — template

*Target length: ~100 words. Replace the bracketed placeholders with
your actual venues, years, and one-line framing. Keep the final
sentence as written — it positions you as an "interactive sculpture"
artist rather than a "tech" artist, which is the single most important
framing decision for this panel.*

---

[FIRST NAME LAST NAME] is a San Francisco–based artist working in
interactive sculpture and light-based installation. Their work has
been shown at [VENUE 1, YEAR] and [VENUE 2, YEAR], and often begins
with a simple question about attention: who is watching whom, and
what changes when the watching becomes visible. They build every piece
by hand from raw components, treating electronics the way a sculptor
treats clay — as a material that rewards patience and repetition.
They live and work in [NEIGHBORHOOD], San Francisco.

---

## Notes for filling this in

- **Venue names matter less than you think.** A group show at a
  neighborhood café counts. A window installation at a community
  space counts. The judges just need to see that you have installed
  finished work in public before — that is the single biggest unknown
  for any applicant proposing a technical piece, and naming even one
  venue resolves it.
- **Avoid the words** "software", "Raspberry Pi", "code", "Python",
  "AI", "computer vision", "machine learning". Every one of those
  words makes a ceramicist on the panel stop reading. Use
  "electronics", "sensors", "custom-built", "bespoke", "hand-built".
- **Do mention** any public-facing dimension of prior work: Burning
  Man, street fairs, open studios, bar or café shows, window displays,
  community events. This panel is tied to a bar — they are choosing
  people who can handle a public venue, not a white-cube gallery.
- **Close with place.** Saying where in SF you live is a small detail
  the panel notices — it reinforces the "supporting local artists"
  purpose of the grant.
