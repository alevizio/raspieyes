# The Regulars

## Artist statement

*"The Regulars"* is a wall-hung cluster of twelve watchful eyes. Round,
mismatched, and patient, they are the bar's permanent patrons — the
ones who never leave, never drink, and never look away. Each eye is a
self-contained round display, tuned and built by hand, housed in a
dark wooden frame that reads from across the room as a single strange
portrait with too many pupils.

When a person crosses the room, the eyes follow. Not in unison — each
one turns from its own position on the frame, so an eye near the top
corner sees a different angle than an eye at the center, and the
cluster physically converges on whoever is standing in front of it.
Two people passing each other will watch the eyes hand them off
between looks. A crowded room becomes a kind of silent audience, the
piece quietly picking out the nearest face and holding its gaze.

The work is interested in the soft edge of surveillance — the intimate
attention of regulars at the end of a bar rather than the institutional
attention of a camera in a ceiling. It asks what happens to a
room when everyone in it is being looked at, gently and without
judgment, by something that cannot be looked back at. In a bar, where
we are already performing for strangers, the piece simply makes the
audience visible.

Twelve eyes. One viewer at a time. The regulars are always here.
